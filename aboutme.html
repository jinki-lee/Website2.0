About Me

Too Long; Didn't Read

Jinki Lee is a Digital Experience Developer at ENMAX, completing his 4-month Co-Op term. His experience includes a wide range of corporate matters including creating high-value products, finding efficient business solutions for stakeholders, and generating creative ideas. Prior to finding passion and a keen interest in UX/UI design and full-stack development, Jinki studied Business and Economics at the University of Calgary.

Jinki obtained his B.A. in Economics and a minor in Business Management from UCalgary (2022), where he developed a strong foundation in: advanced microeconomics, macroeconomics, econometrics, global trading systems, monetary theory, strategic business law and marketing. He quickly learned the importance of computer programming, artificial intelligence, automation, user engagement, user/consumer behaviour, and sustainable energy.

Jinki pursued his interest and passion for technology, and is now completing his Diploma in Information Technology, Software Development at the Southern Alberta Institute of Technology. He received the Alberta Advanced Education and Technology Award for outstanding academic achievements, participation in community activities, volunteering with non-government organizations, involvement in extracurricular activities, and showing leadership skills.

He has the strong willingness to learn, and thrive with the prospect of confronting new challenges. All tasks and opportunities are always another experience where Jinki is able to utilize his existing skillset and build on a new one.

Just recently, I graduated from the University of Calgary (April 2022) with a Bachelor of Arts majoring in Economics and a minor in Business Management and Society. I developed a strong foundation in advanced microeconomics, macroeconomics, econometrics, global trading systems, monetary theory, strategic business law and marketing. During my time I was able to quickly learn new applications such as Minitab, and Stata, and further develop my Microsoft Office skills. Using these applications allowed me to collect and present the data I had researched throughout the numerous cost-benefit analysis, research papers, and professional presentations. I also took other interesting option courses such as ART 251 - Media Arts: Practice & Theory. This course introduced me to Adobe Creative Suite, specifically Adobe Photoshop. At this time, I was not sure exactly when and how I would apply this skill to my career, but quite frankly, it has helped me tremendously...

I am now a student at SAIT enrolled in the Information Technology program, majoring in Software Development (May 2022) . I consider myself a tech-savvy individual but had no prior experience in software development. During my undergraduate studies at UCalgary, I contemplated my career path and wanted to pursue a field I was more passionate about. Rather than dropping out or switching out of my program, I decided to finish my Bachelor's degree and enroll at SAIT.

No doubt, this was the best decision I have made in my life as I am now exploring the beginning of my journey as a software developer. After my first year, I have been introduced: object oriented programming, website design, interface design, database design and programming. After barely scratching the surface of these courses, I am realizing there is so much to learn and the potential to do great things. I am not the brightest person, but I like to think that I am a very creative person. In my website design class, I was taught to use HTML / CSS / JavaScript, and BootStrap to create and style my own websites according to the rubric and requirements. In my interface design class, I was taught to use AdobeXD, another application in the Adobe Creative Suite, and was introduced to basic UX / UI principles. I found that many principles and fundamentals from my past experience with Adobe Photoshop had transitioned to AdobeXD, making this course fairly straight-foward and giving me the opportunity to explore areas beyond the scope of this course. I learned a lot from these instructors who were passionate about their career and the success for their students, and I also learned that I found a passion for creating products, engaging with users, and exploring UX / UI design.

Kicking off the new year (January 2023), I begin my first professional career as a software developer at ENMAX. My title was the "Digital Experience Co-Op Student" and I worked to maintain and develop ENMAX.com, while also suggesting a new perspectives and applying my education to current and upcoming projects.

I don't necessarily believe in luck, but I do believe you can put yourself in lucky situtions. I went through years of school, taking a variety of courses, meeting new people, and ultimately - followinwg my passion. I am grateful for the people I have met throughout my career for sharing their ideas, supporting my decisions, allowing me to learn from them, and shaping the person I am today.
