The Boy Scout (Software Development) Principle

Boy scouts have an easily understood philosophy, which is that they should always leave their campsites in a better condition than the state which they found it in. Cleaning away one piece of garbage each, or something equivalent, creates a situation where a visit from a boy scout group, becomes a great experience for everybody else. Developers can learn a lot from this philosophy. [1]


Nathan Relke, Development Operation Analyst & Coop Buddy @ ENMAX

I learned this one from my assigned buddy, Nathan, when I was a Digital Experience Student at ENMAX. He told me this the first day on the job when exploring through the old repository for the ENMAX.com website before launch. Although it was not his assigned job, he remembered the Boy Scout Principle from his mentor, when he was first starting off his professional career, to remind himself this principle and to use best practices.

The Frog Sits Under the Well

Because frogs live at the bottom of a well, they look up from the bottom of the well and see the tiny sky like a lid. Every day so, so they assert that the sky is just the same size.

In that tiny world, frogs think of themselves as Lord, so they do not look at anyone. Never learned more about another environment, another world, for its very narrow vision, low level of understanding. But the frogs are so subjective, arrogant, claiming to be superior to everything else. That has become a habit, its bad habits.

The heavy rain only changes the living situation, not the cause of death of the frog, which is the cause of its pride.

The story advises us, whatever the circumstances, we must strive to learn to broaden our horizons and insights. We not only study at school, but also learn many things in life. School life is vast sea of knowledge and experience. We must overcome our limitations and constantly learn to get high education and vision. [2]


Young So, President @ Flair Flexible Packaging & Family Friend

I learned this one from my close family friend, Young So, when I was in my final year of my undergraduate studies at UCalgary. I was struggling and unsure of what I wanted to puruse in life, and seeked for guidence and a direction from someone who my family and I trusted and is very knowledgable. He occasionally tells this story to students at other universities when invited to be a guest seminar, and personally told me this story when I was invited to tour around Flair's Flexible Packaging facility and see how operations worked on the floor and in the office. He emphasized that school and learning from others is incredibly important, as you begin see how others think and also to think differently yourself. This is one of my many reasons why I decided to go to SAIT after graduating from UCalgary to learn a completely new skill and to be more open minded.
