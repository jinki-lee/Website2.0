More About Me

I was born and raised in Calgary, Alberta.

I am Korean! Unfortunately, I am not fluent at it...

I am working towards getting my AZ-900, Microsoft Azure Fundamentals Certification.


Strengths:

  • Creative: I believe to be a creative person who can make a difference in the incredibly competitive business landscape, by proposing new concepts and ideas, and helping the company to find their niche in a saturated market.
  • Curious:I do think it's great for others to see what people are thinking of these days and how to maybe think differently. That is why I try to be more open minded and try to learn something new everyday.
  • Solving Problems:I always liked solving problems as it is a very rewarding feeling. If someone asks me a question and I do not know the answer to the question, I will try my very best to find them the answer as soon as possible
  • Empathetic:In UX, you address the user's problems, so it's important to put aside your own opinions and accept what you hear and see. Having empathy in design means understanding users: not just their needs, but also their constraints and the context of their situation as a whole. I believe this is a characteristic that I have developed and also inherited from my parents. It has significantly made the work I do easier.
  • Organized:I have always been an organized and tidy individual since I was a kid. I like to have a well organized and tidy workspace, plan out my day, and also plan for the future.

Areas to Improve:

  • Public Speaking:I haven't had great exposure to speaking in front of large groups, but this is something I'm committed to improving over time. I also try to read more books to expand my vocabulary
  • Self-criticism:My greatest weakness is that I'm too critical of myself and often feel like I'm not giving my best, or like I disappoint the people I work with.
  • Competitive:I am a very competitive person and it shows in the hobbies I am active in. Although, it is not necessarily a bad thing, I do believe that it is okay to not always be perfect or try to "out-do" someone or something.
  • Patience:However, I think my impatience is a good thing, because I don't like missing targets. My impatience has also taught me how to improve my negotiation skills and how to retrieve information from sources in innovative ways.
